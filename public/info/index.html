Zug Thugz Rules

The following information is subject to change. Any modifications will be announced through Discord.

Guild Information

Zug Thugz is a semi-casual raiding guild focused on clearing content while maintaining a fun and positive atmosphere. We plan to follow wherever Classic may lead, and form a group that is excited to succeed as a collective.

Guild Ranks

The ranks within the guild and their significance are as follows:

  • Zug Supreme - Guild Master
  • Zug Lord - Members expected to help coordinate and drive progression.
  • Core Zug - Progression raiders who are expected to show up to all raids unless an absence is necessary.
  • Zug Thug - Members who are invited to sign up for raids, but can not be guaranteed a spot.
  • Zug Grunt - Non-60 members who are leveling and participating in the guild community.
  • Zug Boi - Intro rank. Used for Alt and as the default rank for new members.

Guild Raiding

Currently, Raid invites start at 7pm server time on Mondays and Tuesdays and pull is set for 7:30. Off schedule raids such as ZG and AQ20 can be formed ad-hoc and should be treated as such. We expect members attending progression raids to try and bring whatever consumables they can afford. World buffs are not required, but highly encouraged, as they can greatly increase an individual’s performance and clear times. Progression characters should also try and adhere to raid viable specs while content is not quite yet on farm.

Guild Loot System

We use the EPGP loot system. This awards Effort Points (EP) to players who are either in the raid or on standby whenever a boss is killed during a raid. Gear Points (GP) are awarded when a player receives an item during a raid. The value of that item is based on a formula of it's slot, rarity, and item level.

In general, loot will be awarded to the person with the highest Priority Rating (PR) and for which the item is most appropriate for (Main Spec / Best in Slot considerations). PR is determined by dividing EP by GP (EP/GP). While the intention is to stick to distribution via PR as often as possible, members should discuss if an item should go to a person lower in PR if it is vital for them (completes a set, is BiS, etc).

Every Core Zug is expected to have a progression character, that is one class/role that you focus on and bring to progression raids. A 40 person raid which is in the latest content patch is considered to be a progression raid. It is expected that Core Zug Progression characters will have priority on items, especially hard to come by items, as to help the guild to continue to clear new content as it comes out.

Alts are allowed and encouraged, and there are a few ways to go about handling loot for non-progression characters:

  1. You can run seperate EPGP between the two. This means your alt will have it's own PR that is not impacted by your main character, but would still have a lower precedence for gear against Progression characters.
  2. You can consolidate all EP to your main character, but you can not use your PR on the alt to obtain items. This means you can only /roll on an item that no one else is willing to purchase, and you will not receive GP for obtaining the loot.
  3. A one time transfer of EPGP to an alt. This is really only meant in a scenario where a player is deciding that they want to convert their Alt to the main Progression character. This will be a one way transfer and won’t be reversible. We ask that you take into consideration the amount of gear you've obtained and your role in progression before making this decision.

Items that are not raid-viable for a classes main spec will be rolled off after the purchase period ends after each boss. This is intended to allow people to gather off spec items and not waste items, but if an item is something you intend to wear in a raid at some point it must be purchased.

20 person raids do not implement the EPGP system, and it is expected that people cooperate about loot distribution. Progression mains should still be our priority for gear, but this is less strict in these settings. We do ask that BiS items from these raids be prioritized to core progression (ZG Idols, enchants, Hakkar loot etc)

A system is also in the works which will allow members to generate a “buy-list” for upcoming raids. This would not replace EPGP in any way, but rather supplements it by making loot distribution easier. A buy-list is created by each member prior to the opening of a new tier of content. These items are simply the items that a person wants from the raid and intends on gaining GP for. What this allows us to do is ensure people who have designated an item as a must-have for them can get priority on those items when they drop. Players who do not have an item on the list are still free to purchase it if no one who has it on their buy-list requests the item. Past that the item will go to offspec rolls as usual. Development is ongoing of the tool to be used, so when Ellbot finishes it, we may put it to use.

It’s impossible to foresee every situation that might occur during raids. If any situation were to arise where a resolution isn’t immediately clear Zug Supreme and Zug Lords will have a short discussion on the topic and return to the group with a proposed solution. If the general consensus is positive, we will make sure to document the resolution and incorporate it into our system so that future similar situations can be resolved via its precedent. 

Guild Etiquette

We have people from all walks of life in this guild and the top priority is to treat each other with respect. In the end, this is a game. Something we are here to have fun with and take a break from our daily grinds. Behavior considered to be hateful, racist, sexist, homophobic, etc will not be tolerated. We understand that people have different perspectives on life, but repeated unacceptable behavior will be addressed in the way we deem appropriate.

If you decide to invite a friend to the guild, please make sure that their guild note is appropriately updated so that we know who introduced them into the guild.
